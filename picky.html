Picking your Promise Poison

    (or, "how Peter Piper Picked a Peck of Petulant Promise Poison")

    The frameworks shown are by no means the only implementations of promises in Javascript. They are just the two most popular and a peek at how it can be done, for interest' sake

    Of course, the question arises: How do I select the promise library to use?

    Well, as with most tools, pick the one which is most convenient.

    • If you're in a project with only jQuery, use $.Deferred. Just bear in mind you have to catch exceptions and reject() with them, as need be.
    • If you're in a Node application, you probably want Q (or something similar). No point bringing in the whole of jQuery for an environment where the DOM is often irrelevant.
    • If you're in an Angular application, I'd suggest using the inbuilt, slightly cut-down version of Q that is provided with the service $q. The $http service uses it anyway -- and I'd also suggest steering away from using the extended $http methods (fail and success) as they just introduce inconsistency and a potential for confusion
